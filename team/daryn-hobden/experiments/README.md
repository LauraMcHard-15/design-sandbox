# Experiment Brief

Use this template to plan and reflect on an AI experiment. It doesn't need to be long — even a few bullet points per section is enough. The point is to think *before* you prompt, and reflect *after*.

Copy this file into your own `experiments/` folder and rename it to something descriptive (e.g. `experiment-component-descriptions.md`).

---

**Date:**

---

## 1. What am I trying to do?

> Describe the task or problem in one or two sentences. Pretend you're explaining it to a colleague.

---

## 2. Why AI for this?

> What made you think AI might help here? Speed? Ideas? A task you find tedious? There's no wrong answer.

---

## 3. My hypothesis

> What do you *expect* to happen? This doesn't need to be right — it just helps you notice when something surprises you.

---

## 4. What I actually did

> Walk through what you tried. Paste in key prompts, describe the back-and-forth, note any adjustments you made along the way.

---

## 5. Results

> What did you end up with? Screenshots, outputs, or files can go in your `designs/` folder — just reference them here.

---

## 6. Reflection

**What worked well?**

**What didn't work?**

**What would I do differently?**

**Would I use AI for this kind of task again?** Yes / No / Maybe — and why.

---

## 7. Anything worth sharing with the team?

> A prompt that worked really well? A surprising limitation? A workflow tip? If so, consider dropping it in `_shared/resources/`.
